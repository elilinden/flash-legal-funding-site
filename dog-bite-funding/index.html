How Dog Bite Pre-Settlement Funding Works

Dog bite injuries can be physically and emotionally devastating — and the medical bills can add up quickly. Reconstructive surgery, infection treatment, physical therapy, and psychological counseling are common expenses that victims face while waiting for their case to resolve.

Pre-settlement funding from Flash Legal Funding provides a cash advance against your expected dog bite settlement so you can cover medical costs, lost wages, and daily living expenses while your attorney negotiates with the dog owner's insurance company.

Who Qualifies for Dog Bite Funding?

To be eligible for pre-settlement funding on a dog bite case, you generally need:

Dog Bite Funding FAQ

Can I get pre-settlement funding for a dog bite case?

Yes. Dog bite and animal attack cases are eligible for non-recourse pre-settlement funding. As long as you have an attorney and a pending personal injury claim, you can apply for a cash advance against your expected settlement.

How much funding can I receive for a dog bite injury?

Funding amounts are based on the estimated value of your dog bite case. Most plaintiffs receive between 10-20% of the expected settlement. Factors include the severity of your injuries, medical treatment costs, scarring or disfigurement, and the dog owner's insurance coverage.

What if the dog owner doesn't have insurance?

Many dog bite claims are covered under the dog owner's homeowner's or renter's insurance policy. Your attorney can help identify available coverage. If there is a viable path to recovery, you may still qualify for pre-settlement funding.

Do I have to repay the funding if I lose my dog bite case?

No. Our funding is non-recourse, meaning you owe nothing if your case does not result in a settlement or judgment. The financial risk is entirely on us, not on you.
