Rideshare Accident Funding FAQ

Can I get pre-settlement funding for an Uber or Lyft accident?

Yes. If you were injured in a rideshare accident — whether as a passenger, another driver, or a pedestrian — and have a pending personal injury claim with legal representation, you can apply for non-recourse pre-settlement funding.

Why are rideshare accident cases complicated?

Rideshare accident cases often involve multiple insurance policies — the driver's personal policy, the rideshare company's commercial policy, and potentially a third-party driver's coverage. Determining which policy applies depends on whether the driver was logged into the app, en route to a pickup, or actively transporting a passenger. This complexity can extend the timeline for settlement.

How much funding can I receive for a rideshare accident case?

Funding amounts are based on the estimated value of your rideshare accident case. Most plaintiffs receive between 10-20% of the expected settlement. Uber and Lyft carry up to $1 million in liability coverage for active trips, which can support larger funding amounts for serious injuries.

What happens if I lose my rideshare accident case after receiving funding?

Because our funding is non-recourse, you owe nothing if your case is unsuccessful. If your rideshare accident lawsuit does not result in a settlement or judgment, you keep the money and have no obligation to repay.
