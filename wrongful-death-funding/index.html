How Wrongful Death Pre-Settlement Funding Works

Losing a family member to someone else's negligence is devastating — and the financial impact can be immediate. Funeral expenses, lost household income, mortgage payments, and childcare costs don't wait for a lawsuit to settle. Wrongful death cases often take years to resolve due to their complexity and the high stakes involved.

Pre-settlement funding from Flash Legal Funding provides a cash advance against the expected wrongful death settlement so surviving family members can manage expenses while the case moves through litigation.

Because our funding is non-recourse, repayment comes only from the settlement proceeds. If the wrongful death case is unsuccessful, you owe nothing — we absorb the loss entirely.

Who Qualifies for Wrongful Death Funding?

To be eligible for pre-settlement funding on a wrongful death case, you generally need:

  • A pending wrongful death lawsuit filed by an eligible family member or estate representative
  • Legal representation from an attorney or law firm
  • Evidence that the death was caused by another party's negligence or wrongful act
  • A viable claim with identifiable liability and insurance coverage

We do not check your credit score, employment status, or income. Approval is based entirely on the merits and projected value of the wrongful death case.

Wrongful Death Funding FAQ

Can family members get pre-settlement funding for a wrongful death case?

Yes. If you are the surviving spouse, child, parent, or estate representative who has filed a wrongful death lawsuit with legal representation, you can apply for non-recourse pre-settlement funding to help cover immediate financial needs while the case is resolved.

How much funding can I receive for a wrongful death case?

Funding amounts are based on the estimated value of the wrongful death case. Factors include the decedent's age, earning capacity, number of dependents, and the circumstances of the death. Wrongful death cases often carry substantial settlement values.

What types of wrongful death cases qualify for funding?

We fund wrongful death cases arising from car accidents, truck accidents, medical malpractice, workplace accidents, defective products, premises liability, and other acts of negligence. As long as there is a viable claim and legal representation, the case may qualify.

Do I have to repay the funding if the wrongful death case is lost?

No. Our funding is non-recourse, meaning you owe nothing if the case does not result in a settlement or verdict. The financial risk is entirely on us, not on you or your family.
