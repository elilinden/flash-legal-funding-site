How Car Accident Pre-Settlement Funding Works

Car accident lawsuits can take months or even years to settle. During that time, you may be dealing with mounting medical bills, lost wages, vehicle repairs, and everyday living expenses. Pre-settlement funding from Flash Legal Funding provides a cash advance against your expected settlement so you can stay financially stable while your case moves forward.

Because our funding is non-recourse, you only repay from your settlement proceeds. If your car accident case is unsuccessful, you owe nothing — we absorb the loss entirely.

Who Qualifies for Car Accident Funding?

To be eligible for pre-settlement funding on a car accident case, you generally need:

  • A pending car accident personal injury lawsuit or claim

  • Clear or strong liability in the accident

We do not check your credit score, employment status, or income. Approval is based entirely on the merits and projected value of your car accident case.

Car Accident Funding FAQ

How much pre-settlement funding can I get for a car accident case?

Funding amounts are based on the estimated value of your car accident case. Most plaintiffs receive between 10-20% of the expected settlement value. Factors include the severity of your injuries, liability clarity, medical treatment costs, and available insurance coverage.

Do I need a lawyer to get pre-settlement funding for my car accident?

Yes, in most cases you need legal representation. We coordinate with your attorney to review case documents and evaluate the strength of your car accident claim. This helps us determine your eligibility and funding amount.

What happens if I lose my car accident lawsuit after receiving funding?

Because our funding is non-recourse, you owe nothing if your case is unsuccessful. The risk is entirely on us. If your car accident lawsuit does not result in a settlement or judgment, you keep the money and have no obligation to repay.

How fast can I receive car accident pre-settlement funding?

Many applicants receive funding within 24 hours of approval. Once we review your case details with your attorney and approve your application, funds can be sent via bank transfer, check, or wire the same day.
