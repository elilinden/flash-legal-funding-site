How Medical Malpractice Pre-Settlement Funding Works

Medical malpractice lawsuits are among the most complex and time-consuming personal injury cases. They often require extensive expert testimony, medical record review, and multiple depositions — and they can take years to resolve. During that time, you may be facing mounting medical bills, lost income, and ongoing treatment costs.

Pre-settlement funding from Flash Legal Funding provides a cash advance against your expected medical malpractice settlement so you can cover living expenses, medical care, and other costs while your case moves through litigation.

Because our funding is non-recourse, you only repay from your settlement proceeds. If your medical malpractice case is unsuccessful, you owe nothing — we absorb the loss entirely.

Who Qualifies for Medical Malpractice Funding?

To be eligible for pre-settlement funding on a medical malpractice case, you generally need:

  • A pending medical malpractice lawsuit or claim
  • Legal representation from an attorney experienced in malpractice litigation
  • Documented injuries caused by medical negligence
  • Evidence supporting a breach of the standard of care

We do not check your credit score, employment status, or income. Approval is based entirely on the merits and projected value of your medical malpractice case.

Medical Malpractice Funding FAQ

How much pre-settlement funding can I get for a medical malpractice case?

Funding amounts depend on the estimated value of your medical malpractice case. Most plaintiffs receive between 10-20% of the expected settlement value. Factors include the severity of the injury, the strength of your malpractice claim, medical costs, and long-term care needs.

Why do medical malpractice cases take so long to settle?

Medical malpractice claims involve complex medical evidence, expert witness testimony, and often face aggressive defense from hospital insurance carriers. Cases can take two to five years or longer, which is why pre-settlement funding is especially helpful for malpractice plaintiffs who need financial support during litigation.

What happens if I lose my medical malpractice case after receiving funding?

Because our funding is non-recourse, you owe nothing if your case is unsuccessful. If your medical malpractice lawsuit does not result in a settlement or verdict, you keep the money and have no obligation to repay.

What types of medical malpractice cases qualify for pre-settlement funding?

We fund a wide range of medical malpractice cases including surgical errors, misdiagnosis, delayed diagnosis, birth injuries, medication errors, anesthesia mistakes, and failure to treat. As long as you have legal representation and a viable claim, you may qualify for funding.
