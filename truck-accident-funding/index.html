Truck Accident Funding FAQ

Why do truck accident cases take so long to settle?

Truck accident cases are often more complex than typical auto accidents. They involve multiple liable parties (trucking company, driver, manufacturer), federal trucking regulations, extensive discovery, and higher settlement values. Insurance companies and their attorneys aggressively defend these cases, which can extend litigation for years.

How much pre-settlement funding can I get for a truck accident case?

Because truck accident settlements tend to be larger due to the severity of injuries and liability factors, funding amounts can be higher than other case types. We typically advance 10-20% of the estimated case value. The exact amount depends on your injuries, liability, medical treatment, and insurance coverage available.

Is truck accident pre-settlement funding a loan?

No. Pre-settlement funding is a non-recourse cash advance, not a loan. Repayment comes only from your settlement proceeds if you win. If your truck accident case is unsuccessful, you owe nothing. There are no credit checks, no monthly payments, and no personal liability.
