Pre-Settlement Funding vs. Workers' Compensation

It's important to understand the difference. Workers' compensation is a no-fault insurance system that covers medical bills and partial lost wages regardless of who caused the injury. Pre-settlement funding applies to personal injury lawsuits filed against third parties responsible for your injury — such as a negligent contractor, equipment manufacturer, or property owner.

If you have a third-party personal injury claim in addition to a workers' comp claim, you may be eligible for pre-settlement funding on the personal injury portion of your case.

Workplace Injury Funding FAQ

Can I get pre-settlement funding for a workplace injury?

Yes, if you have filed a personal injury lawsuit related to a workplace injury and have legal representation, you may qualify for non-recourse pre-settlement funding. This applies to third-party liability claims where someone other than your employer is responsible for your injury.

Is workplace injury pre-settlement funding different from workers' compensation?

Yes. Pre-settlement funding applies to personal injury lawsuits filed against third parties, not to workers' compensation claims. For example, if you were injured by defective equipment made by a third-party manufacturer, or hurt at a construction site due to another contractor's negligence, you may file a personal injury lawsuit in addition to any workers' comp claim.

What types of workplace injuries qualify for funding?

We fund a wide range of workplace injury cases including construction site accidents, industrial machinery injuries, toxic exposure, falling objects, scaffolding collapses, warehouse accidents, and more. The key factors are clear liability against a third party, documented injuries, and active legal representation.
