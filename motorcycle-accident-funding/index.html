Why Motorcycle Accident Victims Need Pre-Settlement Funding

Motorcycle accidents often result in far more severe injuries than car accidents. Without the protective frame of a vehicle, riders are exposed to direct impact, road rash, and ejection injuries. Recovery from these injuries can take months or years, leaving you unable to work and facing mounting bills.

Making matters worse, insurance companies frequently try to lowball motorcycle accident claims — or shift blame onto the rider. Your attorney needs time to build a strong case and negotiate for full compensation. Motorcycle Accident Funding FAQ

Can I get pre-settlement funding after a motorcycle accident?

Yes. If you have a pending motorcycle accident personal injury lawsuit and legal representation, you may qualify for non-recourse pre-settlement funding. We evaluate your case based on liability, injury severity, and available insurance coverage — not your credit score.

Why are motorcycle accident settlements often larger?

Motorcycle riders lack the structural protection of a car, which means motorcycle accidents frequently result in more severe injuries — broken bones, road rash, spinal injuries, and traumatic brain injuries. More serious injuries typically mean higher medical costs, longer recovery times, and greater settlement values.

Will the insurance company's bias against motorcyclists affect my funding?

No. We evaluate your case objectively based on the merits — liability, documented injuries, and medical treatment. We understand that insurance companies sometimes try to shift blame to motorcyclists. Our underwriting focuses on the facts of your case, not biases.
